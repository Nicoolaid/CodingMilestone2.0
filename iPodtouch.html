iPod Touch

The iPod Touch, launched in 2007, was a revolutionary device that combined the features of an iPod with many capabilities of an iPhone, minus the cellular phone functionality. It featured a touchscreen interface, access to the App Store, and Wi-Fi connectivity, making it a versatile device for music, apps, and internet browsing. Additionally, the iPod Touch included a built-in camera, enabling users to take photos and record videos. Over the years, it saw multiple upgrades, improving its hardware and software capabilities. The device became popular among users who wanted the experience of an iPhone without the need for a phone plan, appealing especially to younger audiences and those looking for a multifunctional media player
